How to Pay Off $10,000 in Credit Card Debt in 12 Months

A realistic, month-by-month plan for eliminating high-interest credit card debt as fast as possible.

Paying off $10,000 in credit card debt in 12 months sounds aggressive ‚Äî and it is. But it's completely achievable with a concrete plan. The math requires roughly $900‚Äì$1,100 per month in debt payments, depending on your interest rate. Here's exactly how to get there, month by month.

$10,000
eliminated in 12 months = ~$833/month principal + interest. Here's the roadmap.

First: Know Your Numbers

Before making a single extra payment, calculate your exact situation. You need four numbers:

  • Total balance: Every card, added up.
  • Weighted average interest rate: The blended rate across all your cards.
  • Current minimum payments total: What you're paying now.
  • Extra cash available monthly: After true essential expenses.

At 20% APR on $10,000, you're paying roughly $167/month in interest alone. That means of every $900 payment, $733 actually reduces your balance. This is why acceleration matters ‚Äî every extra dollar in month 1 saves you months later.

Where to Find the $900/Month

Most people don't have an extra $900 sitting around ‚Äî they have to engineer it. Here's the breakdown of how most people reach this number:

  • Cut subscriptions & recurring expenses: Average household has $200‚Äì$300 in cuttable subscriptions (streaming, gym, boxes, apps).
  • Reduce food costs: Cooking at home 5 days vs. 3 = $150‚Äì$250/month saved.
  • Pause investing temporarily: Pausing 401k contributions above the employer match for 12 months is controversial but mathematically sound if your card rate exceeds your expected investment return.
  • Side income: Even $200‚Äì$400/month from freelancing, delivery, or selling items changes everything.
  • Tax refund lump sum: Average US refund is $3,000 ‚Äî if that arrives mid-year, you could be done in 9 months.

The Three Rules You Cannot Break

  1. Stop using the cards entirely. Even one charge breaks the momentum and adds to the target. Freeze them, delete stored card info, use cash or debit only.
  2. Don't miss a payment, ever. A missed payment adds a late fee, can spike your APR to 29.99%, and damages your credit score. Automate minimums immediately.
  3. Build a $500‚Äì$1,000 mini emergency fund first. Without a cushion, any unexpected expense goes back on the card. One week of hustle before you start can fund this buffer.

üìå Key Insight

Every month you delay starting costs you ~$167 in interest (at 20% APR). Starting today ‚Äî even with an imperfect plan ‚Äî beats waiting for a perfect plan. Perfection is not the goal. Progress is.
