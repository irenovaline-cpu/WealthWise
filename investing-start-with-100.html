How to Start Investing With Just $100: A Complete Beginner's Guide

You don't need thousands to start. Here's exactly what to do with your first $100 in the market today.

‚è± 10 min read ¬∑ By WealthWise Team ¬∑ February 2025 ¬∑ Free
The single biggest barrier to investing isn't money ‚Äî it's the belief that you don't have enough to start. You do. $100 invested today begins your compounding journey. $100 invested at 22 is worth dramatically more than $10,000 invested at 42. Here's exactly where to put your first $100.

Step 1: Choose Your Platform (5 Minutes)

Open a free account on one of these platforms ‚Äî all support $100 or less to start:

M1 Finance: Best for building a portfolio of stocks/ETFs. No commissions, fractional shares, automatic investing. Minimum: $100 for taxable accounts, $500 for IRA.

Fidelity: Best for beginners who want a full-featured platform. No minimums, zero-commission index funds, excellent customer service. Their ZERO funds have 0% expense ratio.

Acorns: Best if you struggle to save. Links to your bank and invests spare change automatically. $3/month fee is only justified once your balance exceeds $3,000+.

Schwab: Great all-around platform with $0 minimums and fractional shares (Schwab Stock Slices). Excellent for beginners wanting access to advisor help later.

Step 2: Open the Right Account Type (10 Minutes)

For most beginners: open a Roth IRA first. Contributions are after-tax but all growth is tax-free forever. If you earn under $146,000 (single) or $230,000 (married) in 2024, you qualify.

Why Roth first? At a young age, you're likely in a low tax bracket. Pay taxes now (at 12-22%) so you never pay taxes again on those gains ‚Äî even if your portfolio grows to $1,000,000+. The tax-free compounding over 40+ years is extraordinarily valuable.

If your employer offers a 401k with matching contributions: contribute enough to get the full match before opening an IRA. That's free money ‚Äî always take it first.

Step 3: What to Buy With Your $100

With $100, buy one of these ‚Äî you don't need to think harder than this:

VTI (Vanguard Total Stock Market ETF): Owns 4,000+ US companies. One purchase, instant diversification across the entire US economy. Expense ratio 0.03%. This is the 'set and forget' investment for beginners.

FZROX (Fidelity ZERO Total Market): If using Fidelity, this fund tracks the same index as VTI but with 0% expense ratio. Free investing.

SCHB (Schwab US Broad Market ETF): Schwab's equivalent to VTI at 0.03%. Great if you're on Schwab's platform.

All three are essentially identical investments. Pick the one on your chosen platform and buy it. Stop overthinking. Start investing.

Step 4: Set Up Automatic Monthly Contributions

The most important step isn't the first $100 ‚Äî it's the next $50, and the $50 after that, every single month. Set up an automatic transfer from your bank to your investment account on the same day as your paycheck arrives.

Start with whatever you can afford ‚Äî even $25/month. Increase it by $10 each quarter. The habit is worth more than the amount. Most platforms allow you to automate this completely. Once it's set up, your wealth builds on autopilot every month without any further action from you.

$100 invested today + $50/month for 40 years at 7% = $131,000. That's the math of starting small but starting now.
