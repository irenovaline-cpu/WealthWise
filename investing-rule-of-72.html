The Rule of 72: The Mental Math Trick Every Investor Should Know

Divide 72 by your expected return. That's how many years to double your money. Use it everywhere.

‚è± 5 min read ¬∑ By WealthWise Team ¬∑ February 2025 ¬∑ Free
The Rule of 72 is the simplest, most useful mental math formula in personal finance. It tells you how many years it takes to double your money at any given rate of return ‚Äî and you can do it in your head in 5 seconds. Once you know it, you'll use it constantly.

The Formula

Years to double = 72 √∑ Annual Return Rate

Examples: At 6% annual return ‚Äî 72 √∑ 6 = 12 years to double. At 8% return ‚Äî 72 √∑ 8 = 9 years to double. At 12% return ‚Äî 72 √∑ 12 = 6 years to double. At 1% (traditional savings account) ‚Äî 72 √∑ 1 = 72 years to double.

The math is approximate but remarkably accurate. The actual number for 6% is 11.9 years. The Rule of 72 gives you 12. Close enough for any planning purpose.

How to Use It for Investing Decisions

The Rule of 72 instantly reveals why investment fees matter so much. A fund earning 8% doubles every 9 years. A fund earning the same 8% but charging a 1% fee earns 7% net ‚Äî doubling every 10.3 years. That 1.3 year difference compounding over 30 years costs you enormous amounts.

It also shows why high-interest debt is so dangerous. Credit card debt at 20% APR doubles every 3.6 years. A $5,000 balance you ignore becomes $10,000 in 3.6 years, then $20,000 in another 3.6 years. The same math that builds wealth through investing destroys it through debt.

Apply It to Real Life Decisions

Comparing savings accounts: Your bank offers 0.5% APY. Ally Bank offers 4.25% APY. Using Rule of 72: 72 √∑ 0.5 = 144 years to double at your bank. 72 √∑ 4.25 = 17 years to double at Ally. Same $10,000. Completely different outcomes.

Evaluating compound growth timelines: Starting at 25 vs 35 with a 7% return. At 25: money doubles at 35, 45, 55, 65 ‚Äî four doublings. $10,000 becomes $160,000. At 35: doubles at 45, 55, 65 ‚Äî three doublings. $10,000 becomes $80,000. A 10-year head start at 7% literally doubles your final outcome.

The Inverse Rule of 72

You can also use it backwards: if you want to double your money in 8 years, what return do you need? 72 √∑ 8 = 9% annual return. In 10 years? 72 √∑ 10 = 7.2%. In 6 years? 72 √∑ 6 = 12%.

This helps you reality-check investment promises. Someone claiming 50% annual returns is promising to double your money every 1.4 years. Historically, the best investors on earth ‚Äî Warren Buffett, Peter Lynch ‚Äî have averaged 20-25% over decades. 50% sustained returns don't exist legitimately. The Rule of 72 helps you spot BS quickly.
