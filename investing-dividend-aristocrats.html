7 Dividend Aristocrats That Have Raised Payouts for 25+ Years

These companies have increased dividends through recessions, pandemics, and market crashes. Here's why.

‚è± 11 min read ¬∑ By WealthWise Team ¬∑ February 2025 ¬∑ Free
A Dividend Aristocrat is a company in the S&P 500 that has increased its dividend payment every single year for at least 25 consecutive years. Through the dot-com crash, the 2008 financial crisis, and COVID ‚Äî these companies never cut and never stopped raising. They are the gold standard of dividend investing.

Why Dividend Aristocrats Work

Raising dividends requires consistent profitability and cash flow. Companies that have done it for 25+ years have proven they can generate cash through any economic environment. This durability is exactly what dividend investors are paying for.

Research from S&P Global shows that Dividend Aristocrats have historically outperformed the broader S&P 500 with lower volatility. You get better risk-adjusted returns because you're selecting the most financially durable companies on earth.

The 7 Stocks to Know

Johnson & Johnson (JNJ) ‚Äî 60+ years of consecutive increases. Healthcare giant with pharmaceutical, medical device, and consumer health divisions. Yield: 3.1%. The definition of defensive dividend stock.

Coca-Cola (KO) ‚Äî 60+ years of increases. Warren Buffett's largest personal holding for decades. Global brand moat, pricing power, and emerging market growth. Yield: 3.2%.

Procter & Gamble (PG) ‚Äî 66+ years of increases. Sells essential consumer goods (Tide, Pampers, Gillette) in 180+ countries. People buy these products regardless of economic conditions. Yield: 2.4%.

Realty Income (O) ‚Äî Monthly dividend payer with 25+ years of increases. Triple-net lease REIT with 11,000+ properties. Nicknamed 'The Monthly Dividend Company.' Yield: 5.5%.

3M (MMM) ‚Äî 64+ years of increases. Industrial conglomerate with 60,000+ products across healthcare, safety, and consumer segments. Yield: 6.8%.

AbbVie (ABBV) ‚Äî Pharmaceutical giant that inherited Abbott Labs' 40+ year streak. HUMIRA sales declining but diversified pipeline replacing revenue. Yield: 3.9%.

PepsiCo (PEP) ‚Äî 50+ years of increases. Snacks (Frito-Lay) plus beverages create more stable revenue than Coke. International growth runway. Yield: 3.0%.

What to Look for Before Buying

Payout ratio: This is the percentage of earnings paid as dividends. A ratio below 60% is generally safe. Above 80% means the dividend could be at risk if earnings decline. Check this before any dividend stock purchase.

Dividend growth rate: A stock yielding 3% that grows its dividend 8% annually will yield 6.5% on your original investment in 10 years (yield on cost). Dividend growth matters as much as current yield.

Free cash flow coverage: The dividend must be covered by free cash flow, not just earnings. Companies can report earnings without generating actual cash. FCF coverage ratio above 1.5x indicates a safe dividend.

How to Build Around These 7

Don't buy all 7 equally. Build position sizes based on your conviction and sector balance. A portfolio overly concentrated in consumer staples (KO, PG, PEP) has less diversification than it appears.

Consider adding REITs (O), healthcare (JNJ, ABBV), and industrials (MMM) alongside consumer staples for true sector diversification. Then supplement with ETFs like SCHD (Schwab US Dividend Equity ETF) for broader dividend exposure across 100+ dividend stocks automatically.
