Can Acorns Round-Ups Really Build Wealth? We Ran the Numbers

How much does $0.30/day actually add up to over 10, 20, and 30 years? The math is surprising.

‚è± 7 min read ¬∑ By WealthWise Team ¬∑ February 2025 ¬∑ Free
Acorns markets itself as the app that turns spare change into investments. Round up your $3.70 coffee to $4.00, and $0.30 goes into your investment account. It sounds almost too small to matter. But we ran the actual math ‚Äî and the results surprised us.

The Average Acorns User's Round-Ups

Acorns reports the average user generates about $30-50/month in round-ups from everyday purchases. If you spend $25/day on 3-5 transactions, you'll round up an average of $1-2 per transaction ‚Äî roughly $30-60 in round-ups per month.

We'll use $40/month ($0.30/day average) as our baseline. That's the spare change from your morning coffee, lunch, and a couple of other purchases each day. It feels negligible. Over time, it's not.

The 10, 20, and 30 Year Numbers

Assuming $40/month invested and a 7% average annual return (consistent with long-term S&P 500 performance):

10 years: $6,976 total. You contributed $4,800. Market growth added $2,176. Not life-changing, but meaningful.

20 years: $20,533 total. You contributed $9,600. Market growth added $10,933. Growth now exceeds your contributions.

30 years: $48,173 total. You contributed $14,400. Market growth added $33,773. Your money has more than tripled your contribution. This is purely from round-ups ‚Äî money you were spending anyway and never noticed.

Add a $5/Week Recurring Investment

Acorns lets you add recurring investments on top of round-ups. Adding just $20/month ($5/week) to your $40 in round-ups brings your total to $60/month. Over 30 years at 7%: $72,000. You contributed $21,600. Growth created $50,400 ‚Äî more than double your contributions.

This is the real power of Acorns: it creates an investment habit for people who couldn't save before. The round-ups are psychological training wheels. Once you see your balance growing, you naturally increase your contributions.

Is Acorns Worth the $3/Month Fee?

At small balances, the fee hurts. On a $500 balance, $36/year represents 7.2% of your balance ‚Äî wiping out your returns. Acorns only becomes cost-effective once your balance exceeds approximately $3,000-4,000, where the $36 annual fee drops below 1% of your balance.

Our verdict: Use Acorns to build the habit and reach $3,000-5,000. Then consider moving to a commission-free platform like M1 Finance for larger balances. The behavioral value of Acorns ‚Äî making investing automatic and invisible ‚Äî is genuinely worth $3/month for beginners who would otherwise not invest at all.
