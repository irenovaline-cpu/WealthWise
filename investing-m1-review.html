M1 Finance Review: The Best Free Investing App for Beginners in 2025

Fractional shares, automatic DRIP, zero commissions. Everything a new investor needs in one app.

‚è± 6 min read ¬∑ By WealthWise Team ¬∑ February 2025 ¬∑ Free
M1 Finance has quietly become one of the best investing platforms for long-term investors. No commissions, no management fees, fractional shares, and automatic dividend reinvestment ‚Äî all completely free. Here's our full review.

How M1 Finance Works

M1 uses a concept called Pies. You create a Pie ‚Äî a visual representation of your portfolio ‚Äî and assign target percentages to each stock or ETF you want to own. Deposit money, and M1 automatically buys shares proportionally to keep your Pie balanced.

For example: 40% VTI, 20% VXUS, 15% BND, 25% dividend stocks. Deposit $500, and M1 buys $200 of VTI, $100 of VXUS, $75 of BND, and $125 spread across your dividend picks ‚Äî automatically, with fractional shares, so every dollar is invested.

What Makes It Stand Out

Automatic DRIP: Every dividend payment is automatically reinvested into your Pie proportionally. No action required. This is the most powerful long-term wealth builder and M1 makes it effortless.

Fractional shares: Own 0.001 shares of Amazon if you want. Your full contribution is invested ‚Äî no money sitting idle in cash because you couldn't afford a full share.

Zero commissions and zero management fees: The free tier costs nothing. Compare this to a robo-advisor like Betterment charging 0.25% annually ‚Äî on $100,000, that's $250/year you keep with M1.

IRA support: Open a Roth IRA or Traditional IRA directly in M1, with the same Pie investing system.

The Limitations to Know

One daily trading window: Free users get one trading window per day (9:30 AM EST). You can't execute trades at specific prices throughout the day. For long-term investors making monthly contributions, this is completely irrelevant.

No cryptocurrency: M1 doesn't offer crypto trading. Use a separate platform if you want crypto exposure.

No tax-loss harvesting: M1 Premium ($3/month) adds a second trading window but still doesn't offer automated tax-loss harvesting. For that, look at Betterment or Wealthfront.

Customer service: Support is email-based and can be slow. Not ideal for urgent account issues.

Our Verdict

M1 Finance earns a 5/5 rating for long-term, passive investors building dividend portfolios or index fund portfolios. The automatic Pie investing, free DRIP, and fractional shares make it the best free tool available for this strategy.

We use M1 Finance for our own dividend portfolio and recommend it to every beginner building long-term wealth. Open an account, build your Pie, set up automatic monthly deposits, and let M1 run it ‚Äî completely hands-free.
