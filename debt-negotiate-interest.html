How to Negotiate Lower Interest Rates on Your Credit Cards (Script Included)

A single 10-minute phone call using this exact script can save you thousands in interest charges.

üí≥ Debt-Free Series¬∑WealthWise Editorial Team

Here's something most credit card companies don't advertise: you can call and ask for a lower interest rate ‚Äî and it works more often than you'd think. Studies show that more than 60% of people who ask for a rate reduction receive one. The average person never asks. The ten-minute phone call can save you $500‚Äì$3,000 in interest over your payoff period.

When This Works Best

  • You've had the card for at least 6 months
  • You have a clean payment history (no recent late payments)
  • Your credit score has improved since you opened the card
  • You're a long-standing customer with the bank
  • You have competing offers (either from balance transfer cards or other issuers)

üí° Best time to call: Tuesday through Thursday, between 10am‚Äì2pm. Customer service reps have more flexibility mid-week and mid-day. Avoid Monday mornings and Friday afternoons.

Before You Call: Do This

  1. Know your current APR on the card
  2. Know your credit score (Credit Karma or your bank's free tool)
  3. Have competing offers ready to mention (a balance transfer offer you received, or another card's rate)
  4. Know your on-time payment history with this card

The Exact Script to Use

When Connected to Representative: "Hi, I've been a customer with [Bank] for [X years] and I have a great payment history with you. I'm currently looking at my finances and I'd like to see if there's any flexibility on my APR. I've received some competing balance transfer offers at lower rates, and I'd prefer to keep my business with you. Is there anything you can do to lower my interest rate?" If They Say Yes: "Thank you so much. Can I confirm: my new rate will be [X]%? And when will that take effect?" If They Say No: "I understand. Is there a supervisor or specialist I could speak with who might have more flexibility? Or could you note in my account that I called to request a rate review?" If Still No: "I appreciate you looking into this. I may need to transfer the balance to take advantage of better offers, but I'd like to remain a customer. Is there any other way you can help ‚Äî like waiving my annual fee or offering a balance transfer at a promotional rate?"

After the Call

If you got a rate reduction: ask them to send written confirmation by email or mail. Check your next statement to confirm the new rate is applied. Recalculate your payoff timeline with the new, lower rate ‚Äî you'll finish faster.

If they said no: don't give up. Call back in 3 months. Or proceed with a balance transfer. The "no" costs you nothing and you've lost only 10 minutes. The "yes" could save you thousands.

Set a 10-minute block on your calendar this week. Pull up each credit card's customer service number. Call them one by one. This is arguably the highest hourly "rate" of any action you can take ‚Äî potentially $500‚Äì$3,000 saved for 30 minutes of work.

‚úÖ Your Action Steps

  1. List every credit card with its current APR and customer service phone number.
